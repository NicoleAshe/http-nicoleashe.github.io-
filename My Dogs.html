Not too long later, my parents decided to get a dog from the rescue center. One dog in particular caught the eyes of my little brother and I, and we ended up taking a pre-named dog named Krystal who had been abused by her owner and was rescued from a flood. We still don't know what breed she is; she is definitely a mix of a few things. Krystal became my best friend; she protected me, including growling at anyone she didn't trust around me, or if she thought someone who was trying to mess with me was actually trying to hurt me). She would sleep with me every single night. One time, I went to summer camp for a week. My mom sent me a letter, as there was no service, and told me that Krystal would wait by my bedroom door every night waiting for me to let her in, and would eventually give up and go to bed, sad and confused. It broke and warmed my heart at the same time. Another time, my parents made me record our phone message, and sometimes I would hide somewehere and call the house phone and bring it to the voicemail. Krystal would hear it and would wine as she ran around the house until she found me, and she would get so happy. It was the cutest thing ever.

Sammy and Krystal are best friends now; Krystal bullies him, but it's obvious they wouldn't know what to do without each other. They're both getting a little old; we're specifcally worried about Sammy, as boxers don't usually live too long anyway. I'm emotionally preparing myself to deal with two difficult losses of my childhood and my family, but for now they are a huge part of my excitement each time I come home from college.
